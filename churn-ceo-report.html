Section 02

Why they actually leave

* Multiple causes per client — totals exceed 100%. N = 69 churned clients.
Top Finding
45% of clients left because ads were losing them money. We never had a documented breakeven conversation with 69% of those clients.
This is the single highest-leverage fix available. If every new client has a documented breakeven ROAS on Day 1, and we trigger an alert at Day 10 below breakeven, we catch most performance churn before it becomes a cancellation.
Second Finding
32% of exits cited creative quality. In multiple cases, a client's own organic content outperformed everything we delivered.
AI voiceovers, stock footage, and generic "performance" aesthetics are destroying trust with premium brands. Amen Fragrances' founder edited one TikTok himself and got 100,000 views. We had been producing for months.

Section 05

The 8 fixes that would change everything

01
Breakeven ROAS Conversation at Onboarding
69% of performance churn cases had no documented breakeven. Every new client must complete a profit margin worksheet in Week 1. Their breakeven ROAS gets stored in the account — and triggers an automatic escalation alert if breached for 10+ consecutive days.
Owner: Ops Week 1–2 Critical
02
Pre-Launch QC Checklist (Non-Negotiable)
Wrong landing pages, expired promo copy, archived products in ads, wrong country targeting — these appear in 15+ client cases. A mandatory 5-point check before any ad goes live: LP verified, product in stock, promo active, tracking live, copy market-specific.
Owner: All PMs Week 1–2 Critical
03
Brand-Tier Creative Standard (No AI Default)
AI voiceovers and stock footage are destroying trust with premium clients. At onboarding, classify every client as Mass Market or Premium. Premium clients require human voiceovers, authentic footage, and brand style guide compliance before delivery. AI voice requires explicit client sign-off.
Owner: Creative Leads Week 2–4 High
04
Team Transition Handover Protocol
Quality drops on 8 engagements following PM or media buyer changes. Minimum 2-week overlap. Mandatory: (1) written brand brief, (2) creative style guide, (3) handover call with client present, (4) first delivery under new PM reviewed by outgoing PM before sending.
Owner: Mary / Ops Week 2–3 High
05
Account Manager-Owned Retention (Not Bruno)
Bruno was consistently involved only after clients had already decided to leave. AMs must own retention. Rule: if a client misses 2+ meetings OR goes 14+ days without a message, the AM escalates proactively — not waits to be asked. Build this into weekly reporting.
Owner: Ciaran / Bruno Week 1 Critical
06
Cancellation Billing Stop — Immediate
5+ clients were charged after verbal cancellation, creating hostile exits and dispute threats. Rule: any verbal or written cancellation signal instantly triggers a billing freeze confirmation within 24 hours. No exceptions. Billing disputes convert neutral exits into hostile ones.
Owner: Finance / Bruno Week 1 Critical
07
Pre-Sign Business Viability Check
Good Day Optics (losing $8k/month), Little Big Playroom (revenue down 67%), Truck Parts (CA$240/day profit after all costs) — these were doomed from Day 1. Add a pre-sign checklist: YoY revenue trend, gross margin, breakeven ROAS calculation. Any client needing >8x ROAS to break even requires leadership approval.
Owner: Sales / Ciaran Week 2–4 High
08
Prove We Beat Their Best Ad
Tropeaka cancelled because "Linear ads aren't outperforming our in-house ads." Amen Fragrances' founder got 100k TikTok views on a video he made himself. Every full-service engagement should include a formal A/B test in Month 2: Linear's top creative vs. client's historical best. Own the benchmark — don't let them discover it themselves.
Owner: Growth Managers Month 1–2 Medium
