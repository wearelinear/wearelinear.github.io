Meta and Shopify Audit • Nov 2025 vs Nov 2024

Executive Summary

Performance from Nov 1-26 2024 versus the same period in 2025 shows a clear decline in ROAS and MER. The biggest driver of this drop is the change in Conversion Rate. Other metrics such as CTR and AOV have also declined, but those losses are offset by cheaper CPMs. The core of the problem is the drop in purchases relative to adds to cart.

Objective: Understand why CVR dropped and define what can be done to reverse it.

Key Insight

The largest driver of the ROAS decline is the drop in Conversion Rate. Ad performance has not meaningfully degraded and CPMs are cheaper this year. The ads are still producing strong Add to Cart volume. The issue sits deeper in the funnel.

3. Interpretation

The higher ROAS in Nov 2024 is almost entirely due to abnormal open funnel activity on Shopify that inflated conversion rates by allowing customers to skip normal checkout steps.

The performance difference is fully explained by abnormal funnel behavior in 2024. Ad performance in 2025 is not the problem.

2025 Reality Check

  • CTR is only slightly lower
  • CPM is cheaper
  • ATC rate is significantly higher

Question for the Client:

Do you remember anything unusual during Nov 2024 that could explain this behavior? (e.g., New apps, Shop Pay tests, Email flows with direct checkout links, Theme bugs). Understanding this helps set the right baseline.

4. Paid Ads Quick Wins

1. Launch Google Ads

Google is a major untapped opportunity. It usually drives higher ROAS and captures high intent users. Just ensure setup avoids cannibalizing branded search and focuses on incremental revenue.

2. Update Meta Structure

The current structure is outdated. After Meta's Andromeda update, best practice is:

  • Large CBO campaigns
  • Fewer ad sets (sometimes just one!)
  • High creative variety inside a single broad campaign
  • Mix of TOF, MOF, BOF creatives together

3. Consolidate UK Accounts

Splitting the same geo across two accounts reduces optimization power. Consolidating into one ad account gives the platform more data and stability.

4. Replace Modular Testing

Small modular variations (hooks, CTAs) no longer produce meaningful learning. Effective testing in 2025/26 requires net new creative concepts:

New Angles New Scripts New Formats

Final Conclusion

The ROAS difference between Nov 2024 and Nov 2025 is driven almost entirely by Shopify funnel behavior, not Meta performance.

Meta continues to drive strong traffic and add to carts. The shift in Conversion Rate results from the disappearance of the open funnel effect that inflated last year's results.

With a few high impact changes on Meta and the immediate launch of Google Ads, performance can be improved and revenue can be scaled moving forward.
